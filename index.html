Augmented Euclideanizing Flows: Stable and Flexible Sensorimotor Learning

Structure of AEF. The conditional normalizing flows model takes the robotic arm's end-effector position $\mathbf{x}_c$ as input and utilizes the representation $\mathbf{h}$ obtained from feature extraction of environmental images ${{\mathbf{x}}_{nc}}$ as conditions. This setup maps the inputs into a latent variable space. Within this space, a dynamical system is defined by $\mathbf{\dot{z}}=-\mathbf{z}$, where the velocity is modulated by fully connected layers and ensured to be positive through exponential transformation. Ultimately, the desired end-effector velocity output in task space is achieved by computing the inverse of the Jacobian matrix of latent variable $\mathbf{z}$ concerning ${{\mathbf{x}}_{c}}$, mapping the latent space velocity back to task space.

Abstract

Imitation learning with dynamical systems ensures stability, but often fails in multitask settings with high-dimensional observations. We propose augmented Euclideanizing flows (AEF), a framework that constructs diffeomorphic mappings from complex observations to a shared latent space, enabling stable policy learning across tasks. To improve robustness against various visual variations and to utilize asynchronously collected or unlabeled data, the framework incorporates a constraint on semantic consistency during training. In addition, implicit target learning is achieved by aligning goals with the latent-space origin, removing the need for explicit detection. The approach is supported by theoretical analysis and validated in simulation and real-world robotic experiments, demonstrating stable multitask modeling and effective handling of high-dimensional observations.
